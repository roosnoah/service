About Robert Williams Law

Your choice in Entertainment, Media and Finance



Mr. Williams founded RW Law in 2015 to expand his entertainment practice after over a decade of experience as an attorney at Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom, LLP and Dechert LLP. He brings a wealth of knowledge to each deal, leveraging his hands-on experience with complex transactions. Since September 2019, Mr. Williams is “Of Counsel” to Schroder Brooks Law Firm PLC, where he provides specialized advice from Munich on entertainment matters. Schroder Brooks also services clients of Mr. Williams.

Mr. Williams advises on film and television development, production, financial structuring, distribution and licensing. He also focuses on music-related agreements that intersect film and television (e.g. synchronization licenses, master use licenses, composer agreements, sound track album license agreements and co-publishing and admin agreements). Mr. Williams has a robust practice representing lenders on loan facilities, secured transactions and general corporate matters.

Mr. Williams authored a treatise on motion picture financing in The Essential Guide to Entertainment Law: Dealmaking. He has lectured multiple times at Bucerius Law School in Hamburg and most recently at Soundtrack Cologne in August 2019.




Mr. Williams has significant complex transactional experience and project management know-how, gained from his work at Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom, LLP and Dechert LLP. Having managed large capital markets, leveraged finance and cross border M&A transactions, in 2011 Mr. Williams began using his finance experience in the area of single-picture financing structures. He now focuses on advising clients at each step of the motion picture and television process, including development, co-production, production, finance and licensing. He also advises with respect to music agreements, writer and director agreements, the guilds (WGA, DGA and SAG-AFTRA) and on corporate law.

Mr. Williams has lectured multiple times at Bucerius Law School, Hamburg. At the University of Virginia School of Law he served as Articles Review Editor for both the Virginia Tax Review and the Virginia Journal of International Law.

Mr. Williams has logged hundreds of hours of pro bono time, including successfully arguing the case of an asylum applicant as first chair in Federal Immigration Court. He also served for over a year as a member of the board of directors of REC Music Foundation, a non-profit that seeks to elevate lesser known operatic or classical works.
